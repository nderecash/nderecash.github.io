## The problem

Gaming events are rarely built for disabled players. Accessibility, where it
exists, is an afterthought — a ramp at the door of an experience designed for
someone else. I wanted to test what happens when a tournament is designed
accessible-first: venue, hardware, format, communications, everything.

## What I did

I designed, fundraised and led the tournament end to end, twice.

- Secured **Xbox as sponsor** and **Nokia Arena** as venue — an international
  brand and Finland's flagship arena backing an accessibility-first event.
- Recruited and led the volunteer team; designed the tournament format around
  adaptive controllers and mixed-ability play.
- Ran communications: brand, social media, press, and the live broadcast
  relationship with **Yle Areena**, Finland's national streaming service.

## Results

- **2024:** 100+ attendees in person, a packed venue, streamed live on Yle Areena.
- **2025:** second edition, ~50 in person, again live on Yle Areena.
- The event became an **ethnographic study of accessible esports event design**,
  now under review at Academic Mindtrek 2026 — and a talk at the Spring Game
  Researcher Seminar, Tampere Game Research Lab (May 2026).

## What it proves

Accessibility is my specialism, not a side interest. I can take an idea from
nothing to a sponsored, broadcast, arena-scale event — and then turn the
practice into research.
